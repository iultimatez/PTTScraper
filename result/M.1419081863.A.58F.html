剛看到某調查台灣2014最夯搜尋排行榜 http://focustaiwan.tw/news/asoc/201412200003.aspx 原文如下 On the lighter side: What Taiwan was Googling in 2014 (Dec. 13-19) (簡單翻譯米糕): 台灣2014年都google些啥咪!? The year's not quite over, but Google has already compiled data on what Taiwan was searching for in 2014 -- and the top searches show that Web users were too lazy to type URLs. Google做了些調查看看台灣2014年都google些什麼 而且大家都直接打字都懶的去搜網址 (是說現在還有人在搜網址嗎...!??) The list released last week shows that Taiwan's top search words were "Facebook," "Yahoo," and surprisingly, "Google." That's right, people were opening their Web browsers to the Google page so that they could search for... the Google page. 排行第一 Facebook 排行第二 Yahoo 令人驚訝的排行第三 Google (......) No. 4 was "YouTube," followed by the Chinese name for auction site Ruten, then "weather" (tian qi), "translate" (fan yi), popular discussion board eyny forum (yi li lun tan), and e-commerce site "PChome," with job search website "104" rounding out the top 10. 排名第四 YouTube 第五 露天拍賣 第六 天氣 第七 翻譯 第八 伊莉討論區 第九 PChome 第十 104 In terms of fast-trending searches, "World Cup" (shi jie bei) saw the biggest upsurge in searches by Taiwanese users over the year, reflecting interest in soccer -- a sport that is largely overlooked most of the time. 跟風搜尋最高潮 世界盃 (此反應出被長期忽視的足球這項運動對多數人來說仍有吸引力) Googlers were clearly also concerned with the trade-in-services pact with China, as its Chinese abbreviation (fu mao) came in second on the list, followed by "iPhone 6" in third and its much cheaper competitor, "Xiaomi" (in Chinese, naturally), in fourth. 其它: 服貿 iPhone 6 小米 The list of most popular people searches must have been a hard pill to swallow for Taiwanese entertainers, who gave up the top spots to their South Korean rivals. 人物搜尋方面....韓星奪冠 Heartthrob Kim Soo-hyun -- who plays the dashing alien Do Min-joon in drama series "My Love from the Star" -- was the most likely to beam his way into Taiwanese Web users' search bar, followed by compatrior Lee Min-ho, who starred in the drama "The Heirs." 金秀賢 《來自星星的你裡》 飾演 都敏俊 另一競爭者是 《繼承者們》2014最佳男演員 李敏鎬 Korean pop group Girls Generation was fourth. 少女時代 The only Taiwanese to make the top five were pop-rock band Mayday and the bizarre sub-culture Internet celebrity known as Tong Shen. 樂團方面~ 五月天 奪冠 Taiwan's pop king Jay Chou, who sort of vaguely announced he was getting married this year, may be heartbroken for not even making the list despite that bombshell. 歌手 周杰倫 但搜尋度高的原因是結婚八卦~ In terms of trending searches, one double winner is Ko Wen-je. The underdog surgeon and political independent who managed to win the race for mayor of Taipei also topped the trending search for people, indicating a big jump in searches for his name over the year. 年度搜尋人物 柯文哲 On the trends list, he was followed by Kim Soo-hyun (again!), student protest leader Lin Fei-fan, cartoonist Wan Wan (who saw her popularity suffer when it came to light she was having an affair), and Cheng Chieh, a university student being prosecuted for stabbing nearly 30 people -- four fatally -- on the Taipei Metro in May. 其它人物 金秀賢(又是他!) 學運人物 林飛帆 卡通畫家 彎彎 (也是因為誹聞) 殺人犯 鄭捷 Ultimately, the data corroborate what we already know: many Taiwanese went through 2014 with their heads buried in their iPhones or Xiaomi handsets, focused almost exclusively on largely trifling domestic issues except for when a few handsome Korean men managed to find their ways onto their screen. 最後...
